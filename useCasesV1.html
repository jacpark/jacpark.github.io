The Desert Sparrow is a custom quadcopter.


Use Cases

Use Case 1: Hover

Goal: To be able to hover. Maintain altatude and hold position in space.

Why?: If I can make the quad hover it will be able to keep itself orientated correctly while flying.

Steps:

1: Calibrate MPU6050

2: Start collecting sensor data

3: Turn on motors and increase speed to lift off

4: Use acceleromiter data to maintain orientation

5: At 2 meters off the ground slow motors to maintain altitude

6: Repeat step 4 to maintain hover.

7: Decrease motor speed slightly to decend and land

Use Case 2: Bank(Roll and Pitch)

Goal: Have the quad-copter able to tilt itself left, right, forward, and back.

Why?: Give the quad-copter the ability to move through space.

Steps:

1: Must already be in the air.(See Hover)

2: Decrease the speed of two motors in the direction you want to go.

2A: Increase the speed of the other two motors.

3: Use acceleromiter/gryoscope data to determine pitch or roll angle

3: After reaching the desired angle return motors to hover speed

4: (optional) increase motor speed to increase travel speed.

5: Once you have reached your desired location level quad copter again.

5A: Increase speed of motors that were slowed before and slow the speed of motors that were sped up before

6: Use acceleromiter/gryoscope data to determine pitch or roll angle

7: Return motors to hover speed and maintain hover

Use Case 3: Spin(Yaw)

Goal: While hoving in place the quad should be able to spin clockwise or counterclockwise.

Why?: So that the front of the quad can be pointed in the direction you want to go or looking at something you want to interact with.

Steps:

1: Must already be in the air.(See Hover)

2: Decrease speed of two diagonal motors while increaseing speed of other two diagonal motors

2A: This will keep your quad hovering level but will cause it to spin in the direction of the increased motors.

3: Once you've reached your new heading return motors to hover speed.

Use Case 4: Collect saguaro cactus fruit

Goal: Fly to the top of a saguaro cactus and pick the fruit so I can eat it.

Why?: For the past three years of living here those fruits have tantalized me every time I go for a hike. Now I have the technology to aquire them.

Steps:

1: Begin flight and hover(see hover)

2: Navagate to cactus (see bank and spin)

3: Hover near cactus, then increase all four motors to reach the top.

4: Return to hover in position at the top of the cactus.

5: Pick the fruit. I am thinking the easiest method would just to have a spike that could be propelled forward and stab a fruit.

6: Increase motor speed to lift the fruit free.

7: Navigate back to pilot. (see bank and spin).

8: Slowly decrease motor speed to land and deliver the prize!
